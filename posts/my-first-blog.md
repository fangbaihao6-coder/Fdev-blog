# Post 1: Understanding the Problem and Defining Core Requirements

## Initial Project Idea

For my web app prototype, I want to create a study buddy matching app for university students.

The goal of this application is to help students find other people in the same course, with similar study habits and available times.

I chose this idea because many students struggle to meet classmates outside tutorials or lectures. This can be even more difficult for international students, first-year students, or people who are shy.

At the beginning, I considered several possible ideas:

- a travel reflection app with no likes or comments
- a wellbeing habit tracker for students
- a study buddy matching platform

After comparing these ideas, I decided to focus on the study buddy app because it seems practical, useful, and achievable within the time available.

## Identifying the Problem

The main problem this app tries to solve is that students often find it difficult to meet people to study with.

Currently, students may experience issues such as:

- not knowing anyone in the same class
- feeling uncomfortable asking strangers to study together
- struggling to find people with similar schedules
- finding it hard to stay motivated when studying alone

For example, a first-year student may be taking a difficult course and want someone to revise with before exams, but they may not know who else is in the class or how to contact them.

Because of this, I think there is a need for a web app that makes it easier for students to connect with others.

## Target Users

The main users of this app are university students.

This includes:

- first-year students
- international students
- students in large classes
- students who prefer group study

These users may need:

- an easy way to find people in the same course
- a way to compare schedules and availability
- a simple process for sending study requests
- a platform that feels safe and easy to use

Understanding the target users is important because it helps define what features are most important.

## Core Functional Requirements

At this stage, I think the app must include the following core features:

1. User accounts and profiles
2. A way for students to select their courses
3. A search or matching system for finding study buddies
4. A way to send study requests
5. A page showing accepted study partners

These features are necessary because they directly support the main purpose of the app.

I also thought about some additional features that could improve the experience but are not essential:

- built-in messaging
- group study rooms
- calendar integration
- rating or feedback system

I decided to treat these as optional because they may take more time to develop and are not necessary for the first version of the app.

## Constraints and Risks

One challenge with this idea is that building a matching system could become complicated.

For example, matching students based on courses, schedules, and preferences may require more advanced coding than I currently know.

Because of this, I may simplify the matching process.

Instead of automatically recommending the best study partner, I may allow users to search manually by course and available time.

This would still solve the main problem while keeping the project realistic and achievable.

Another challenge is privacy. Students may not want to share too much personal information.

To solve this, users would only need to share basic details such as their course, preferred study times, and study style.

## Reflection

Overall, this first stage helped me understand what my app needs to do and which features are most important.

The biggest thing I learned is that it is better to focus on a few core features that solve one clear problem instead of trying to build too many advanced features.

In the next stage, I want to explore the structure of the app in more detail, including page layout, navigation, and user flow.
